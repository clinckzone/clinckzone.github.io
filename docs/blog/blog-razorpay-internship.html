Work

You‚Äôll not only be made aware about your role in a project but also how you‚Äôll be impacting the entire organisation. Learnings

Before I came to Razorpay, I used to practice what I call an intuition-led design process i.e. my design decisions were led by my intuition instead of explicit logic. Though my intuition was more or less good and my solutions okay-ish, this practice limited me in a number of ways:

  • I was only able to think of design solutions that were already in existence. Hence wherever I saw similar situations popping up I would simply apply solutions that I was already familiar with.
  • I wasn‚Äôt exactly able to back up my decisions with verbal logic. My intuition always gave me good enough solutions but none of it came out from a series of logical statements and conclusions that could be used to defend my solutions.
  • This affected the uniqueness and originality of my solutions because to be able to come up with an original solution, you always need to go back to the fundamentals of a problem and work your way up from there.

I learned to ask ‚Äúwhy‚Äù to every decision that I took. This prevented me from getting biased by my intuition and approach a problem from a logical perspective. I learned to constantly ask ‚Äúwhy‚Äù to land upon a fundamental truth and work my way up from there in a series of logical steps to arrive at a solution that‚Äôs optimized for a particular need. You can apply this model to any decision-making process.

My time at Razorpay has made me more sure of my love towards ‚ÄúBuilding‚Äù. I was really lucky that my Design Lead gave me the opportunity to work on a project where I got to use my creativity as well as intellect. However upon reflection, I feel my current career choice to be a dead end. I realise that although as an intern I got numerous learning opportunities, I would soon get bored of it with increasing years of experience in the industry. I know that I want a career in 'technology' but the current roles available within the industry don't seem to fit my interest at all. One thing that might keep me interested in such a job role can be the vision of the company that I am working with. This isn't just the only reason. Designing or developing applications for screen based interfaces seems very limiting to me. I do agree that one is required to think critically, logically and creatively while building apps for such an interface, but there's only so much one can do with such a design space. Eventually I will want to move out of this for more creative freedom. I am as a result starting to consider HCI research as serious solution to my career crisis.
